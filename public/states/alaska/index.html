Alaska Residency Requirement

There is no minimum residency duration required to file for divorce in Alaska. The petitioner must be a resident of Alaska at the time of filing.

Source: Alaska Court System – Self-Help: Divorce

Alaska Divorce Waiting Period

Alaska requires a mandatory 30-day waiting period from the date the respondent is served before a divorce can be finalized.

Source: Alaska Court System – Self-Help: Divorce

Alaska Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $250. Additional fees may apply for service of process and other court costs.

Source: Alaska Court System – Self-Help: Divorce

Alaska Filing Fee Waiver

Alaska courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Exemption from Filing Fee application to request a waiver of court filing fees.

Source: Alaska Court System – Self-Help: Divorce

Alaska Fault Type

Alaska allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. The no-fault ground is incompatibility of temperament.

Parenting Classes

Alaska does not have a statewide parenting class requirement, but individual courts may order parenting education when minor children are involved.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Alaska

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Alaska?

To file for divorce in Alaska, you must meet specific residency criteria. There is no minimum residency duration required to file for divorce in Alaska. The petitioner must be a resident of Alaska at the time of filing.

How long does a divorce take in Alaska?

The timeline for finalizing a divorce in Alaska depends on mandatory waiting periods and court processing. Alaska requires a mandatory 30-day waiting period from the date the respondent is served before a divorce can be finalized.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Alaska?

Filing for divorce in Alaska involves court fees that vary by county. Filing fees are approximately $250. Additional fees may apply for service of process and other court costs.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Alaska?

Individuals who cannot afford court fees in Alaska may be eligible for a fee waiver. Alaska courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Exemption from Filing Fee application to request a waiver of court filing fees.
