California Divorce Forms & Filing Requirements

California divorce laws require specific formatting, disclosures, and filing procedures. Our California divorce form kits are structured to match court expectations and reduce avoidable rejection issues.

California Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have lived in California for six months and in the county of filing for at least three months before filing for divorce.

Source: California Courts – Divorce Overview

California Divorce Waiting Period

California requires a mandatory six-month waiting period from the date the respondent is served before a divorce can be finalized.

Source: California Courts – Divorce Overview

California Divorce Filing Fees

Typical filing fees range from $435, though additional local court fees may apply depending on the county.

Source: California Courts – Divorce Overview

California Filing Fee Waiver

California courts allow eligible individuals to request a fee waiver based on income and financial circumstances using official Judicial Council forms.

Source: California Courts – Divorce Overview

California Fault Type

California is a no-fault divorce state. The most common ground is irreconcilable differences.

Parenting Classes

Parenting classes are not required statewide, but some counties may require mediation or parenting orientation when minor children are involved.
