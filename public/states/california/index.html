How to File for Divorce in California (Step-by-Step Guide)

Filing for divorce in California requires meeting residency rules, completing state-specific forms, and following court filing procedures. Below is a general overview of the uncontested divorce process.

Step 1 – Meet California Residency Requirements

At least one spouse must have lived in California for six months and in the filing county for at least three months before filing.

Step 2 – Complete Required California Divorce Forms

California requires specific court-approved forms. Using state-specific divorce documents helps reduce formatting errors and rejection risk.

Step 3 – File Forms With the Court

Divorce petitions are filed with the Superior Court in the appropriate county. Filing fees typically range between $250 and $435 depending on jurisdiction.

Step 4 – Serve Your Spouse

The filed paperwork must be formally served on the other spouse according to California service requirements.

Step 5 – Exchange Financial Disclosures

California requires both spouses to complete mandatory financial disclosures before a divorce can be finalized.

Step 6 – Finalize the Divorce Judgment

After required waiting periods and paperwork review, the court may issue a final judgment of dissolution.

How Long Does Divorce Take in California?

California has a mandatory six-month waiting period from the date the responding spouse is served before a divorce can be finalized. Processing time may vary by county.

How Much Does Divorce Cost in California?

Costs typically include court filing fees, service expenses, and document preparation costs. Attorney representation can significantly increase total expenses.

Can Filing Fees Be Waived in California?

Individuals who meet income requirements may request a fee waiver from the court. Approval is determined by the reviewing judge.
