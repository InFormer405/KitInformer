Colorado Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been domiciled in Colorado for at least 91 days before filing for dissolution of marriage.

Source: Colorado Judicial Branch – Self-Help: Divorce

Colorado Divorce Waiting Period

Colorado requires a mandatory 91-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed and the respondent is served before a decree of dissolution can be entered.

Source: Colorado Judicial Branch – Self-Help: Divorce

Colorado Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $230, though fees may vary by judicial district. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Source: Colorado Judicial Branch – Self-Help: Divorce

Colorado Filing Fee Waiver

Colorado courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a Motion to File Without Payment to request a waiver of court filing fees.

Source: Colorado Judicial Branch – Self-Help: Divorce

Colorado Fault Type

Colorado is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for dissolution is that the marriage is irretrievably broken.

Parenting Classes

Colorado requires both parents to complete a court-approved parenting class when minor children are involved in a dissolution proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Colorado

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Colorado?

Before initiating divorce proceedings in Colorado, residency standards must be met. At least one spouse must have been domiciled in Colorado for at least 91 days before filing for dissolution of marriage.

How long does a divorce take in Colorado?

After filing, Colorado imposes a required waiting period before the court can issue a final divorce decree. Colorado requires a mandatory 91-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed and the respondent is served before a decree of dissolution can be entered.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Colorado?

The cost of filing for divorce in Colorado includes mandatory court fees. Filing fees are approximately $230, though fees may vary by judicial district. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Colorado?

If filing fees present a financial hardship, Colorado courts may grant a waiver. Colorado courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a Motion to File Without Payment to request a waiver of court filing fees.
