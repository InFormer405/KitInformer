Delaware Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Delaware for at least six months before filing for divorce.

Source: Delaware Courts – Family Court: Divorce

Delaware Divorce Waiting Period

Delaware does not impose a mandatory post-filing waiting period for uncontested divorces. However, for no-fault divorce, the parties must have been separated for at least six months before filing.

Source: Delaware Courts – Family Court: Divorce

Delaware Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $160 to $200 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Source: Delaware Courts – Family Court: Divorce

Delaware Filing Fee Waiver

Delaware courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.

Source: Delaware Courts – Family Court: Divorce

Delaware Fault Type

Delaware is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for divorce is that the marriage is irretrievably broken.

Parenting Classes

Delaware requires both parents to complete a court-approved parenting education program when minor children are involved in a divorce proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Delaware

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Delaware?

Delaware courts require proof of residency before accepting a divorce filing. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Delaware for at least six months before filing for divorce.

How long does a divorce take in Delaware?

Delaware divorce proceedings follow a mandatory timeline set by state statute. Delaware does not impose a mandatory post-filing waiting period for uncontested divorces. However, for no-fault divorce, the parties must have been separated for at least six months before filing.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Delaware?

Divorce filing costs in Delaware are set by the court system and may vary by jurisdiction. Filing fees are approximately $160 to $200 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Delaware?

Delaware provides a process for requesting a waiver of divorce filing fees. Delaware courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.
