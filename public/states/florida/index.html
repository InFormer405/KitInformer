Florida Divorce Forms & Filing Requirements

Florida divorce laws require specific formatting, disclosures, and filing procedures. Our Florida divorce form kits are structured to match court expectations and reduce avoidable rejection issues.

Florida Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Florida for six months before filing for dissolution of marriage.

Source: Florida Courts – Family Law Self-Help

Florida Divorce Waiting Period

Florida requires a mandatory 20-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a final hearing can be held. Courts may waive this period in certain circumstances.

Source: Florida Courts – Family Law Self-Help

Florida Divorce Filing Fees

Typical filing fees range from $400 to $410, though additional fees may apply depending on the county and type of filing.

Source: Florida Courts – Family Law Self-Help

Florida Filing Fee Waiver

Florida courts allow individuals who meet financial eligibility requirements to apply for a determination of civil indigent status to waive filing fees.

Source: Florida Courts – Family Law Self-Help

Florida Fault Type

Florida is a no-fault divorce state. The only ground for dissolution is that the marriage is irretrievably broken.

Parenting Classes

Florida requires both parents to complete an approved parenting course when minor children are involved in a dissolution of marriage proceeding.
