Florida Fault Type

Florida is a no-fault divorce state. The only ground for dissolution is that the marriage is irretrievably broken.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Florida

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Florida?

Before initiating divorce proceedings in Florida, residency standards must be met. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Florida for six months before filing for dissolution of marriage.

How long does a divorce take in Florida?

After filing, Florida imposes a required waiting period before the court can issue a final divorce decree. Florida requires a mandatory 20-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a final hearing can be held. Courts may waive this period in certain circumstances.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Florida?

The cost of filing for divorce in Florida includes mandatory court fees. Typical filing fees range from $400 to $410, though additional fees may apply depending on the county and type of filing.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Florida?

If filing fees present a financial hardship, Florida courts may grant a waiver. Florida courts allow individuals who meet financial eligibility requirements to apply for a determination of civil indigent status to waive filing fees.
