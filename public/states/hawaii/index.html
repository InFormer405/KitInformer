Hawaii Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Hawaii for at least six months before filing for divorce. The petition must be filed in the circuit where the petitioner resides.

Source: Hawaii State Judiciary – Family Court: Divorce

Hawaii Divorce Waiting Period

Hawaii does not impose a mandatory waiting period after filing for an uncontested divorce. However, contested cases may require additional time for court scheduling.

Source: Hawaii State Judiciary – Family Court: Divorce

Hawaii Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $200 to $275 depending on the circuit. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Source: Hawaii State Judiciary – Family Court: Divorce

Hawaii Filing Fee Waiver

Hawaii courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.

Source: Hawaii State Judiciary – Family Court: Divorce

Hawaii Fault Type

Hawaii is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for divorce is that the marriage is irretrievably broken.

Parenting Classes

Hawaii does not have a statewide parenting class mandate, but individual family courts may order parenting education programs when minor children are involved.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Hawaii

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Hawaii?

Hawaii requires that certain residency conditions be satisfied before a divorce petition can be filed. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Hawaii for at least six months before filing for divorce. The petition must be filed in the circuit where the petitioner resides.

How long does a divorce take in Hawaii?

Hawaii law establishes a specific timeframe that must pass before a divorce becomes final. Hawaii does not impose a mandatory waiting period after filing for an uncontested divorce. However, contested cases may require additional time for court scheduling.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Hawaii?

Court filing fees are required when submitting divorce paperwork in Hawaii. Filing fees are approximately $200 to $275 depending on the circuit. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Hawaii?

Hawaii courts offer fee waiver options for those who meet financial eligibility criteria. Hawaii courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.
