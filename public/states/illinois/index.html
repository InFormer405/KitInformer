Illinois Divorce Forms & Filing Requirements

Illinois divorce laws require specific formatting, disclosures, and filing procedures. Our Illinois divorce form kits are structured to match court expectations and reduce avoidable rejection issues.

Illinois Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Illinois for at least 90 days before filing for dissolution of marriage.

Source: Illinois Courts – Dissolution of Marriage

Illinois Divorce Waiting Period

Illinois requires that the parties live separate and apart for a continuous period of six months before the court may enter a judgment of dissolution. However, this requirement may be waived by agreement of the parties.

Source: Illinois Courts – Dissolution of Marriage

Illinois Divorce Filing Fees

Typical filing fees range from $250 to $388 depending on the county. Cook County fees tend to be higher than other jurisdictions.

Source: Illinois Courts – Dissolution of Marriage

Illinois Filing Fee Waiver

Illinois courts allow individuals who qualify based on income or public benefits to apply for a fee waiver using an Application for Waiver of Court Fees.

Source: Illinois Courts – Dissolution of Marriage

Illinois Fault Type

Illinois is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for dissolution is irreconcilable differences.

Parenting Classes

Illinois requires parents to complete a parenting education program in cases involving minor children, unless the court waives the requirement.
