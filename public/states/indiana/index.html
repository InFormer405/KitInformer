Indiana Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Indiana for at least six months and a resident of the county of filing for at least three months before filing for dissolution.

Source: Indiana Judiciary – Self-Service Legal Center

Indiana Divorce Waiting Period

Indiana requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a final dissolution decree can be entered.

Source: Indiana Judiciary – Self-Service Legal Center

Indiana Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees typically range from $150 to $200 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Source: Indiana Judiciary – Self-Service Legal Center

Indiana Filing Fee Waiver

Indiana courts allow individuals who qualify based on income to file a verified petition for waiver of filing fees.

Source: Indiana Judiciary – Self-Service Legal Center

Indiana Fault Type

Indiana is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for dissolution is an irretrievable breakdown of the marriage.

Parenting Classes

Indiana does not have a statewide parenting class requirement, but many counties require parents to attend a parenting coordination or education program when minor children are involved.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Indiana

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Indiana?

Indiana courts require proof of residency before accepting a divorce filing. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Indiana for at least six months and a resident of the county of filing for at least three months before filing for dissolution.

How long does a divorce take in Indiana?

Indiana divorce proceedings follow a mandatory timeline set by state statute. Indiana requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a final dissolution decree can be entered.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Indiana?

Divorce filing costs in Indiana are set by the court system and may vary by jurisdiction. Filing fees typically range from $150 to $200 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Indiana?

Indiana provides a process for requesting a waiver of divorce filing fees. Indiana courts allow individuals who qualify based on income to file a verified petition for waiver of filing fees.
