Iowa Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Iowa for at least one year before filing for dissolution of marriage, unless the other spouse is a resident of Iowa and was personally served in Iowa.

Source: Iowa Judicial Branch – Divorce Information

Iowa Divorce Waiting Period

Iowa requires a mandatory 90-day waiting period from the date the petition is served before a dissolution decree can be entered.

Source: Iowa Judicial Branch – Divorce Information

Iowa Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $185 to $265 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Source: Iowa Judicial Branch – Divorce Information

Iowa Filing Fee Waiver

Iowa courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed Without Payment of Fees to request a waiver of court filing fees.

Source: Iowa Judicial Branch – Divorce Information

Iowa Fault Type

Iowa is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for dissolution is that the marriage has broken down and there is no reasonable likelihood of reconciliation.

Parenting Classes

Iowa requires parents to complete a court-approved Children in the Middle program or similar parenting education course when minor children are involved.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Iowa

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Iowa?

To file for divorce in Iowa, you must meet specific residency criteria. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Iowa for at least one year before filing for dissolution of marriage, unless the other spouse is a resident of Iowa and was personally served in Iowa.

How long does a divorce take in Iowa?

The timeline for finalizing a divorce in Iowa depends on mandatory waiting periods and court processing. Iowa requires a mandatory 90-day waiting period from the date the petition is served before a dissolution decree can be entered.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Iowa?

Filing for divorce in Iowa involves court fees that vary by county. Filing fees are approximately $185 to $265 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Iowa?

Individuals who cannot afford court fees in Iowa may be eligible for a fee waiver. Iowa courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed Without Payment of Fees to request a waiver of court filing fees.
