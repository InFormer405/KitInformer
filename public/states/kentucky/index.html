Kentucky Fault Type

Kentucky is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for dissolution is an irretrievable breakdown of the marriage.

Parenting Classes

Kentucky does not have a statewide parenting class mandate, but many courts require parents to attend a parenting education program when minor children are involved.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Kentucky

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Kentucky?

Kentucky courts require proof of residency before accepting a divorce filing. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Kentucky for 180 days before filing for dissolution of marriage.

How long does a divorce take in Kentucky?

Kentucky divorce proceedings follow a mandatory timeline set by state statute. Kentucky requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period from the date the respondent is served before a divorce can be finalized.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Kentucky?

Divorce filing costs in Kentucky are set by the court system and may vary by jurisdiction. Filing fees are approximately $150 to $200 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Kentucky?

Kentucky provides a process for requesting a waiver of divorce filing fees. Kentucky courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a Motion to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.
