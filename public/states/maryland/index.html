Maryland Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Maryland for at least six months before filing. If the grounds for divorce occurred outside Maryland, the residency requirement extends to one year.

Source: Maryland Courts – Divorce Information

Maryland Divorce Waiting Period

Maryland does not impose a mandatory waiting period after filing for an uncontested divorce based on mutual consent. For divorces based on separation, a six-month separation period is required before filing.

Source: Maryland Courts – Divorce Information

Maryland Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $165 to $215 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Source: Maryland Courts – Divorce Information

Maryland Filing Fee Waiver

Maryland courts allow individuals who meet financial eligibility requirements to file a Request for Waiver of Prepaid Costs to waive court filing fees.

Source: Maryland Courts – Divorce Information

Maryland Fault Type

Maryland allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. No-fault grounds include mutual consent and a six-month separation.

Parenting Classes

Maryland requires parents to attend a court-approved parent education program in many counties when minor children are involved in a divorce proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Maryland

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Maryland?

Before initiating divorce proceedings in Maryland, residency standards must be met. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Maryland for at least six months before filing. If the grounds for divorce occurred outside Maryland, the residency requirement extends to one year.

How long does a divorce take in Maryland?

After filing, Maryland imposes a required waiting period before the court can issue a final divorce decree. Maryland does not impose a mandatory waiting period after filing for an uncontested divorce based on mutual consent. For divorces based on separation, a six-month separation period is required before filing.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Maryland?

The cost of filing for divorce in Maryland includes mandatory court fees. Filing fees are approximately $165 to $215 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Maryland?

If filing fees present a financial hardship, Maryland courts may grant a waiver. Maryland courts allow individuals who meet financial eligibility requirements to file a Request for Waiver of Prepaid Costs to waive court filing fees.
