Massachusetts Residency Requirement

If the cause of divorce occurred within the state, either spouse may file in Massachusetts. If the cause occurred outside the state, the filing spouse must have been a resident for at least one year.

Source: Massachusetts Court System – Divorce Information

Massachusetts Divorce Waiting Period

Massachusetts requires a mandatory 120-day waiting period (called a nisi period) after the court grants a divorce judgment before it becomes final.

Source: Massachusetts Court System – Divorce Information

Massachusetts Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $200 to $220 for a joint petition. A complaint for divorce filing fee is approximately $280. Additional surcharges may apply.

Source: Massachusetts Court System – Divorce Information

Massachusetts Filing Fee Waiver

Massachusetts courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Affidavit of Indigency to request a waiver of court filing fees and costs.

Source: Massachusetts Court System – Divorce Information

Massachusetts Fault Type

Massachusetts allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. The no-fault ground is an irretrievable breakdown of the marriage.

Parenting Classes

Massachusetts requires both parents to attend a court-approved parent education program when minor children are involved in a divorce proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Massachusetts

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Massachusetts?

Massachusetts requires that certain residency conditions be satisfied before a divorce petition can be filed. If the cause of divorce occurred within the state, either spouse may file in Massachusetts. If the cause occurred outside the state, the filing spouse must have been a resident for at least one year.

How long does a divorce take in Massachusetts?

Massachusetts law establishes a specific timeframe that must pass before a divorce becomes final. Massachusetts requires a mandatory 120-day waiting period (called a nisi period) after the court grants a divorce judgment before it becomes final.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Massachusetts?

Court filing fees are required when submitting divorce paperwork in Massachusetts. Filing fees are approximately $200 to $220 for a joint petition. A complaint for divorce filing fee is approximately $280. Additional surcharges may apply.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Massachusetts?

Massachusetts courts offer fee waiver options for those who meet financial eligibility criteria. Massachusetts courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Affidavit of Indigency to request a waiver of court filing fees and costs.
