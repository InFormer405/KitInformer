Information Verified: January 2026 Sources: Official State Court Websites Content Type: Informational Resource (Not Legal Advice)

Michigan Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Michigan for 180 days and a resident of the county where filing for at least 10 days before filing for divorce.

Source: Michigan Courts – Divorce Information

Michigan Divorce Waiting Period

Michigan requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period for divorces without minor children, and a six-month waiting period when minor children are involved. The court may shorten the six-month period in certain circumstances.

Source: Michigan Courts – Divorce Information

Michigan Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees typically range from $175 to $255 depending on the county and whether children are involved.

Source: Michigan Courts – Divorce Information

Michigan Filing Fee Waiver

Michigan courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a Fee Waiver Request to waive court filing fees.

Source: Michigan Courts – Divorce Information

Michigan Fault Type

Michigan is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for divorce is that there has been a breakdown of the marriage relationship to the extent that the objects of matrimony have been destroyed.

Parenting Classes

Michigan does not have a statewide parenting class requirement, but many courts require parents to attend a parenting education program before a divorce involving minor children can be finalized.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Michigan

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Michigan?

Michigan courts require proof of residency before accepting a divorce filing. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Michigan for 180 days and a resident of the county where filing for at least 10 days before filing for divorce.

How long does a divorce take in Michigan?

Michigan divorce proceedings follow a mandatory timeline set by state statute. Michigan requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period for divorces without minor children, and a six-month waiting period when minor children are involved. The court may shorten the six-month period in certain circumstances.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Michigan?

Divorce filing costs in Michigan are set by the court system and may vary by jurisdiction. Filing fees typically range from $175 to $255 depending on the county and whether children are involved.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Michigan?

Michigan provides a process for requesting a waiver of divorce filing fees. Michigan courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a Fee Waiver Request to waive court filing fees.
