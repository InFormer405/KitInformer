Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Minnesota

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Minnesota?

Residency eligibility is a prerequisite for filing divorce paperwork in Minnesota. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Minnesota for at least 180 days before filing for dissolution of marriage.

How long does a divorce take in Minnesota?

How quickly a divorce is finalized in Minnesota is governed by statutory waiting requirements. Minnesota does not impose a mandatory waiting period for uncontested dissolutions, but the court may delay proceedings if it finds a reasonable prospect of reconciliation.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Minnesota?

Minnesota courts charge filing fees when a divorce petition is submitted. Filing fees are approximately $365 to $400 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process and other court costs.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Minnesota?

Fee waiver programs are available in Minnesota for eligible individuals filing for divorce. Minnesota courts allow individuals who meet income guidelines to file a Fee Waiver Application (IFP) to request a waiver of court filing fees.
