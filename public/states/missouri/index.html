Missouri Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Missouri for at least 90 days before filing for dissolution of marriage.

Source: Missouri Courts – Self-Represented Litigants: Divorce

Missouri Divorce Waiting Period

Missouri requires a mandatory 30-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a divorce can be finalized.

Source: Missouri Courts – Self-Represented Litigants: Divorce

Missouri Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees typically range from $125 to $225 depending on the county and type of filing.

Source: Missouri Courts – Self-Represented Litigants: Divorce

Missouri Filing Fee Waiver

Missouri courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a Motion to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to waive court filing fees.

Source: Missouri Courts – Self-Represented Litigants: Divorce

Missouri Fault Type

Missouri is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for dissolution is that the marriage is irretrievably broken with no reasonable likelihood of preservation.

Parenting Classes

Missouri requires parents to complete a court-approved parenting education program when minor children are involved in a dissolution proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Missouri

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Missouri?

To file for divorce in Missouri, you must meet specific residency criteria. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Missouri for at least 90 days before filing for dissolution of marriage.

How long does a divorce take in Missouri?

The timeline for finalizing a divorce in Missouri depends on mandatory waiting periods and court processing. Missouri requires a mandatory 30-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a divorce can be finalized.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Missouri?

Filing for divorce in Missouri involves court fees that vary by county. Filing fees typically range from $125 to $225 depending on the county and type of filing.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Missouri?

Individuals who cannot afford court fees in Missouri may be eligible for a fee waiver. Missouri courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a Motion to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to waive court filing fees.
