New Mexico Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been domiciled in New Mexico for at least six months before filing for dissolution of marriage.

Source: New Mexico Courts – Self-Help: Divorce

New Mexico Divorce Waiting Period

New Mexico requires a mandatory 30-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a dissolution decree can be entered.

Source: New Mexico Courts – Self-Help: Divorce

New Mexico Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $130 to $200 depending on the judicial district. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Source: New Mexico Courts – Self-Help: Divorce

New Mexico Filing Fee Waiver

New Mexico courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.

Source: New Mexico Courts – Self-Help: Divorce

New Mexico Fault Type

New Mexico is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for dissolution is incompatibility.

Parenting Classes

New Mexico requires parents to complete a court-approved parenting education program when minor children are involved in a dissolution proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in New Mexico

What are the residency requirements for divorce in New Mexico?

New Mexico courts require proof of residency before accepting a divorce filing. At least one spouse must have been domiciled in New Mexico for at least six months before filing for dissolution of marriage.

How long does a divorce take in New Mexico?

New Mexico divorce proceedings follow a mandatory timeline set by state statute. New Mexico requires a mandatory 30-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a dissolution decree can be entered.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in New Mexico?

Divorce filing costs in New Mexico are set by the court system and may vary by jurisdiction. Filing fees are approximately $130 to $200 depending on the judicial district. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in New Mexico?

New Mexico provides a process for requesting a waiver of divorce filing fees. New Mexico courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.
