North Carolina Divorce Forms & Filing Requirements

North Carolina divorce laws require specific formatting, disclosures, and filing procedures. Our North Carolina divorce form kits are structured to match court expectations and reduce avoidable rejection issues.

North Carolina Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of North Carolina for six months before filing for divorce.

Source: North Carolina Judicial Branch – Divorce Overview

North Carolina Divorce Waiting Period

North Carolina requires a mandatory one-year separation period during which the spouses must live separate and apart before either party may file for absolute divorce.

Source: North Carolina Judicial Branch – Divorce Overview

North Carolina Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees typically range from $225 to $250, though fees may vary by county. Additional service fees may apply.

Source: North Carolina Judicial Branch – Divorce Overview

North Carolina Filing Fee Waiver

North Carolina courts allow individuals who qualify based on income to file a Petition to Proceed as an Indigent to waive court filing fees.

Source: North Carolina Judicial Branch – Divorce Overview

North Carolina Fault Type

North Carolina allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. The no-fault ground requires one year of continuous separation.

Parenting Classes

North Carolina requires parents involved in custody or visitation disputes to attend a court-approved parenting education program in most judicial districts.
