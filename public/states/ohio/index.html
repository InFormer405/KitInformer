How to File for Divorce in Ohio

Filing divorce paperwork in Ohio generally follows these steps. Detailed instructions are included with each kit.

  • Complete the required Ohio divorce forms included in your kit
  • File the Petition and Summons with your local Ohio Superior Court
  • Serve copies of the filed documents on your spouse
  • Wait for the mandatory six-month waiting period under Ohio Family Code Section 2339
  • If uncontested, submit your final judgment paperwork to the court

Ohio requires a minimum six-month waiting period from the date of service before a divorce can be finalized. Court filing fees vary by county; however, a fee waiver request form is included with every kit at no additional charge.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Ohio?

The court filing fee for a Ohio divorce petition is currently $435 in most counties, though this may vary. If you cannot afford the filing fee, you may qualify for a fee waiver — the request form is included free in every InFormer kit.

Can I file for divorce in Ohio without a lawyer?

Yes. Ohio allows individuals to file for dissolution of marriage without an attorney. This is commonly known as filing "pro se" or "in pro per." Our Ohio divorce forms and instructions are designed specifically for self-represented filers.

What is the difference between the No Children and With Children kits?

The No Children kit includes the core Ohio divorce forms needed when no minor children are involved. The With Children kit adds custody, visitation, and child support forms required by Ohio courts when minor children are part of the proceedings.
