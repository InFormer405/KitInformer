Oklahoma Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Oklahoma for six months before filing for divorce. The petition must be filed in the county where either spouse has resided for at least 30 days.

Source: Oklahoma State Courts Network – Self-Help: Divorce

Oklahoma Divorce Waiting Period

Oklahoma requires a mandatory 10-day waiting period for divorces without minor children and a 90-day waiting period when minor children are involved.

Source: Oklahoma State Courts Network – Self-Help: Divorce

Oklahoma Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $180 to $250 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Source: Oklahoma State Courts Network – Self-Help: Divorce

Oklahoma Filing Fee Waiver

Oklahoma courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.

Source: Oklahoma State Courts Network – Self-Help: Divorce

Oklahoma Fault Type

Oklahoma allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. The no-fault ground is incompatibility.

Parenting Classes

Oklahoma requires both parents to attend a court-approved parenting education course when minor children are involved in a divorce proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Oklahoma

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Oklahoma?

Before initiating divorce proceedings in Oklahoma, residency standards must be met. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Oklahoma for six months before filing for divorce. The petition must be filed in the county where either spouse has resided for at least 30 days.

How long does a divorce take in Oklahoma?

After filing, Oklahoma imposes a required waiting period before the court can issue a final divorce decree. Oklahoma requires a mandatory 10-day waiting period for divorces without minor children and a 90-day waiting period when minor children are involved.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Oklahoma?

The cost of filing for divorce in Oklahoma includes mandatory court fees. Filing fees are approximately $180 to $250 depending on the county. Additional fees may apply for service of process.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Oklahoma?

If filing fees present a financial hardship, Oklahoma courts may grant a waiver. Oklahoma courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.
