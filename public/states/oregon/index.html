Oregon Residency Requirement

If the marriage took place in Oregon, there is no residency requirement. If the marriage took place outside Oregon, at least one spouse must have been a resident for six months before filing.

Oregon Fault Type

Oregon is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for dissolution is irreconcilable differences.

Parenting Classes

Oregon requires parents to complete a court-approved parenting education program in many counties when minor children are involved in a dissolution proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Oregon

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Oregon?

To file for divorce in Oregon, you must meet specific residency criteria. If the marriage took place in Oregon, there is no residency requirement. If the marriage took place outside Oregon, at least one spouse must have been a resident for six months before filing.

How long does a divorce take in Oregon?

The timeline for finalizing a divorce in Oregon depends on mandatory waiting periods and court processing. Oregon does not impose a mandatory waiting period for dissolution of marriage proceedings.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Oregon?

Filing for divorce in Oregon involves court fees that vary by county. Filing fees are approximately $280 to $310. Additional fees may apply for service of process and other court costs.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Oregon?

Individuals who cannot afford court fees in Oregon may be eligible for a fee waiver. Oregon courts allow individuals who meet income guidelines to file a Petition for Deferral or Waiver of Filing Fees and Court Costs.
