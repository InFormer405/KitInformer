Pennsylvania Divorce Forms & Filing Requirements

Pennsylvania divorce laws require specific formatting, disclosures, and filing procedures. Our Pennsylvania divorce form kits are structured to match court expectations and reduce avoidable rejection issues.

Pennsylvania Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a bona fide resident of Pennsylvania for at least six months before filing for divorce.

Source: Pennsylvania Courts – Self-Representation

Pennsylvania Divorce Waiting Period

Pennsylvania requires a 90-day waiting period from the date of service of the divorce complaint before the case can proceed on a no-fault basis with mutual consent. Without mutual consent, a one-year separation period is required.

Source: Pennsylvania Courts – Self-Representation

Pennsylvania Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees vary by county, typically ranging from $200 to $350. Philadelphia County fees tend to be on the higher end.

Source: Pennsylvania Courts – Self-Representation

Pennsylvania Filing Fee Waiver

Pennsylvania courts allow individuals who meet income guidelines to file a Petition to Proceed In Forma Pauperis (IFP) to waive court filing fees.

Source: Pennsylvania Courts – Self-Representation

Pennsylvania Fault Type

Pennsylvania allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. No-fault grounds include mutual consent (after 90 days) or a two-year separation.

Parenting Classes

Pennsylvania does not have a statewide parenting class requirement, but many counties require parents to attend a parenting education program when minor children are involved.
