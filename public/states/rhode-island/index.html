Rhode Island Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $160 to $200. Additional fees may apply for service of process and court costs.

Source: Rhode Island Judiciary – Family Court: Divorce

Rhode Island Filing Fee Waiver

Rhode Island courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.

Source: Rhode Island Judiciary – Family Court: Divorce

Rhode Island Fault Type

Rhode Island allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. The no-fault ground is irreconcilable differences that have caused the breakdown of the marriage.

Parenting Classes

Rhode Island does not have a statewide parenting class requirement, but the Family Court may order parenting education when minor children are involved.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Rhode Island

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Rhode Island?

Residency eligibility is a prerequisite for filing divorce paperwork in Rhode Island. At least one spouse must have been domiciled in Rhode Island for at least one year before filing for divorce.

How long does a divorce take in Rhode Island?

How quickly a divorce is finalized in Rhode Island is governed by statutory waiting requirements. Rhode Island requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period after a decision is entered by the Family Court before the divorce becomes final (known as the nisi period).

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Rhode Island?

Rhode Island courts charge filing fees when a divorce petition is submitted. Filing fees are approximately $160 to $200. Additional fees may apply for service of process and court costs.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Rhode Island?

Fee waiver programs are available in Rhode Island for eligible individuals filing for divorce. Rhode Island courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file an Application to Proceed In Forma Pauperis to request a waiver of court filing fees.
