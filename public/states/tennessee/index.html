Tennessee Residency Requirement

At least one spouse must have been a resident of Tennessee for at least six months before filing. If the grounds for divorce arose outside of Tennessee, the residency requirement is extended to one year.

Source: Tennessee State Courts – Divorce Information

Tennessee Divorce Waiting Period

Tennessee requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period for divorces without minor children and a 90-day waiting period when minor children are involved.

Source: Tennessee State Courts – Divorce Information

Tennessee Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees typically range from $250 to $350 depending on the county. Additional service and processing fees may apply.

Source: Tennessee State Courts – Divorce Information

Tennessee Filing Fee Waiver

Tennessee courts allow individuals who cannot afford filing fees to file a Uniform Civil Affidavit of Indigency to request a waiver of court costs.

Source: Tennessee State Courts – Divorce Information

Tennessee Fault Type

Tennessee allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. The no-fault ground is irreconcilable differences.

Parenting Classes

Tennessee requires both parents to complete a court-approved parenting education course when minor children are involved in a divorce proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Tennessee

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Tennessee?

Residency eligibility is a prerequisite for filing divorce paperwork in Tennessee. At least one spouse must have been a resident of Tennessee for at least six months before filing. If the grounds for divorce arose outside of Tennessee, the residency requirement is extended to one year.

How long does a divorce take in Tennessee?

How quickly a divorce is finalized in Tennessee is governed by statutory waiting requirements. Tennessee requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period for divorces without minor children and a 90-day waiting period when minor children are involved.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Tennessee?

Tennessee courts charge filing fees when a divorce petition is submitted. Filing fees typically range from $250 to $350 depending on the county. Additional service and processing fees may apply.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Tennessee?

Fee waiver programs are available in Tennessee for eligible individuals filing for divorce. Tennessee courts allow individuals who cannot afford filing fees to file a Uniform Civil Affidavit of Indigency to request a waiver of court costs.
