How to File for Divorce in Texas (Step-by-Step Guide)

Filing for divorce in Texas requires meeting residency rules, completing state-specific forms, and following court filing procedures. Below is a general overview of the uncontested divorce process.

Step 1 – Meet Texas Residency Requirements

In Texas, at least one spouse must have lived in the state for six months and in the filing county for at least 90 days before filing.

Step 2 – Complete Required Texas Divorce Forms

Texas requires specific court-approved forms. Using state-specific divorce documents helps reduce formatting errors and rejection risk.

Step 3 – File Forms With the Court

Divorce petitions are filed with the District Court in the appropriate county. Texas divorce filing fees typically range between $250 and $350 depending on the county.

Step 4 – Serve Your Spouse

The filed paperwork must be formally served on the other spouse according to Texas service requirements.

Step 5 – Complete the Waiting Period

Texas requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period from the date the divorce petition is filed before a divorce can be finalized.

Step 6 – Finalize the Divorce Decree

After required waiting periods and paperwork review, the court may issue a final decree of divorce.

How Long Does Divorce Take in Texas?

Texas requires a mandatory 60-day waiting period from the date the divorce petition is filed before a divorce can be finalized. Processing time may vary by county.

How Much Does Divorce Cost in Texas?

Costs typically include court filing fees, service expenses, and document preparation costs. Attorney representation can significantly increase total expenses.

Can Filing Fees Be Waived in Texas?

Individuals who meet income requirements may request a fee waiver from the court. Approval is determined by the reviewing judge.
