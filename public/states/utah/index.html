Utah Divorce Forms & Filing Requirements

Last Verified: February 2026

Utah divorce laws require specific formatting, disclosures, and filing procedures. Our State-Specific Divorce Forms for Utah are structured to match court expectations and reduce avoidable rejection issues.

Utah Residency Requirement

The petitioner must have been a resident of Utah and the county of filing for at least three months before filing for divorce.

Source: Utah Courts – Divorce Information

Utah Divorce Waiting Period

Utah requires a mandatory 30-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a divorce decree can be entered. The court may waive this period for good cause.

Source: Utah Courts – Divorce Information

Utah Divorce Filing Fees

Filing fees are approximately $325 to $360. Additional fees may apply for service of process and other court costs.

Source: Utah Courts – Divorce Information

Utah Filing Fee Waiver

Utah courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a Motion to Waive Fees to request a waiver of court filing fees.

Source: Utah Courts – Divorce Information

Utah Fault Type

Utah allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. The no-fault ground is irreconcilable differences.

Parenting Classes

Utah requires both divorcing parents to complete a mandatory divorce education class when minor children are involved, prior to the entry of a divorce decree.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Utah

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Utah?

Utah courts require proof of residency before accepting a divorce filing. The petitioner must have been a resident of Utah and the county of filing for at least three months before filing for divorce.

Utah divorce proceedings follow a mandatory timeline set by state statute. Utah requires a mandatory 30-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed before a divorce decree can be entered. The court may waive this period for good cause.
