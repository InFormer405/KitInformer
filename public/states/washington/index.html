Washington Residency Requirement

The petitioner must be a resident of Washington State at the time of filing. There is no minimum residency duration required before filing.

Washington Divorce Waiting Period

Washington requires a mandatory 90-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed and served before a divorce can be finalized.

Washington Filing Fee Waiver

Washington courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a motion and declaration for a waiver of civil fees and surcharges.

Washington Fault Type

Washington is a no-fault divorce state. The sole ground for dissolution is that the marriage is irretrievably broken.

Parenting Classes

Washington requires both parents to complete an approved parenting seminar when minor children are involved in a dissolution proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions About Divorce in Washington

What are the residency requirements for divorce in Washington?

Washington requires that certain residency conditions be satisfied before a divorce petition can be filed. The petitioner must be a resident of Washington State at the time of filing. There is no minimum residency duration required before filing.

How long does a divorce take in Washington?

Washington law establishes a specific timeframe that must pass before a divorce becomes final. Washington requires a mandatory 90-day waiting period from the date the petition is filed and served before a divorce can be finalized.

How much does it cost to file for divorce in Washington?

Court filing fees are required when submitting divorce paperwork in Washington. Filing fees are approximately $314, though fees may vary slightly by county. Additional surcharges may apply.

Can divorce filing fees be waived in Washington?

Washington courts offer fee waiver options for those who meet financial eligibility criteria. Washington courts allow individuals who meet income criteria to file a motion and declaration for a waiver of civil fees and surcharges.
